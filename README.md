    1. Body and Frame Design
The robot body was designed using a Box shape as the main frame.
The head was created using a smaller Box, and the eyes were made using Cylinders.
The design is simple to improve stability and make the model easy to build.



    2. Leg Design
The robot has four legs made from Box shapes.
Each leg consists of two sections to represent the upper and lower leg.
A small base was added to each foot to improve contact with the ground and increase stability.



    3. Number of Joints and Degrees of Freedom
The robot has 4 joints, one joint for each leg.
It has 4 Degrees of Freedom (4 DOF), allowing each leg to move forward and backward.

    4. Motor Selection
Servo motors were selected because they provide accurate and controlled movement.
One servo motor is assumed for each leg.


    5. Initial Torque Estimation
A medium torque servo motor is assumed to be sufficient to move each leg and support the robot’s weight.
No detailed torque calculations were performed because this is a basic mechanical design.



    6. Stability and Center of Gravity
The four legs are evenly distributed around the body to improve balance.
The center of gravity is approximately at the middle of the robot, helping it remain stable while standing.


    7. Proposed Walking Method
The robot uses a quadruped walking gait.
Two diagonal legs move first, followed by the other two legs, providing better balance during walking.



    8. Expected Mechanical Problems
Joint friction may reduce smooth movement.
Poor weight distribution may cause the robot to tip over.
Weak motors may not provide enough force to move the robot.
Incorrect leg alignment may affect stability during walking.
